#### Country Profile

This page shows within-country correlations between PM2.5 and other pollutants.
The x-axis shows the top 3 and bottom 3 PM2.5 countries after cleaning.
The color represents the correlation between PM2.5 and each pollutant inside the selected country.
Blank cells mean that there are not enough paired observations for a reliable correlation.

---

#### Why Some Countries May Not Appear

A country may be missing from some heatmap cells if it does not have enough paired observations.
For example, Ghana may not appear for some pollutant relationships because the app requires at least 3 valid paired observations between PM2.5 and the selected pollutant.
If a country has PM2.5 data but lacks enough matching observations for another pollutant at the same monitoring locations, the correlation cannot be calculated reliably.
Therefore, blank cells do not mean the country was removed from the whole project. They only mean that the specific country-pollutant pair does not have enough valid data for correlation analysis.

---

#### How to Use This Page

Hover over each tile to see the country, pollutant, correlation value, paired observations, and unit.
Click a tile to analyze the relationship between PM2.5 and the selected pollutant within that country.
The regression plot below helps explain whether the relationship is positive, negative, or weak.
