#### Welcome to the Global Air Quality Research Platform

PM2.5 became a widely discussed environmental issue after severe haze episodes in many cities. Beijing is a well-known example. In 2013, Beijing's annual average PM2.5 concentration reached about 89.5 μg/m³, far above China's national annual standard of 35 μg/m³. During the January 2013 pollution episode, the peak daily average PM2.5 level was reported to reach about 569 μg/m³.
These events made people pay closer attention to fine particulate matter, because PM2.5 can enter the human body and create serious health risks.
This website explores PM2.5 pollution, its relationship with other air pollutants, and how pollution patterns differ across countries through interactive visualizations.
You will move through data cleaning, pollutant correlation, country ranking, PM2.5 profiles, global maps, and a GDP-geography radar comparison.
Through this project, we hope to show that PM2.5 is not only a number in a dataset. It is also connected to public health, urban life, environmental inequality, and global responsibility.
